about me

I study how models — and brains — think.

I'm a Master's student in Computer Engineering at NYU Tandon, with research interests spanning Machine Learning, NeuroAI, Natural Language Processing, and Reinforcement Learning. Before NYU, I completed my B.Tech in AI & Data Science at KL University, studying across 12 schools in 4 countries.

Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at NYU's SAVVY Lab, where I apply statistical and computational modeling to multimodal neural and behavioral data — investigating the anticipatory and social-cognitive mechanisms behind stuttering variability using fNIRS and fMRI signals.

I'm drawn to the hard questions at the intersection of AI and neuroscience: when does prompting fail? What can biological connectomes teach us about artificial architectures? How do we build ML systems that are actually reliable in production?

Things I've built

NLP · LLMs · Reasoning GitHub ↗

Inference-Time Reasoning in Instruction-Tuned LLMs

Investigated how inference-time prompting strategies — Zero-Shot, Few-Shot, Chain-of-Thought, and Self-Generated Examples — interact with model architecture and scale in instruction-tuned encoder-decoder models (FLAN-T5).

Built a modular experimental framework with prompt abstractions, ablation studies, and qualitative error analysis across arithmetic, QA, and commonsense reasoning benchmarks. Findings revealed structural limitations of inference-time reasoning, contributing to understanding of when prompting fails to compensate for underlying model weaknesses.

NeuroAI · PyTorch

Connectome-Guided Reservoir Computing

Designed biologically inspired MSS/SNN/ESN reservoir architectures using human and Drosophila connectomes as structural priors. Achieved perfect classification accuracy on structured spike-train manifolds. Collaborated with NeuroAI researchers to explore cross-species computational principles.
