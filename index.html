about me

I study how models — and brains — think.

I'm a Master's student in Computer Engineering at NYU Tandon, with research interests spanning Machine Learning, NeuroAI, Natural Language Processing, and Reinforcement Learning. Before NYU, I completed my B.Tech in AI & Data Science at KL University, studying across 12 schools in 4 countries.

Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at NYU's jackson lab, where I apply statistical and computational modeling to multimodal neural and behavioral data — investigating the anticipatory and social-cognitive mechanisms behind stuttering variability using fNIRS and fMRI signals.

I'm drawn to the hard questions at the intersection of AI and neuroscience: when does prompting fail? What can biological connectomes teach us about artificial architectures? How do we build ML systems that are reliable in production?

Things I've built

NLP · LLMs · Reasoning GitHub ↗

Inference-Time Reasoning in Instruction-Tuned LLMs

Investigated how inference-time prompting strategies — Zero-Shot, Few-Shot, Chain-of-Thought, and Self-Generated Examples — interact with model architecture and scale in FLAN-T5.

Built a modular experimental framework with ablation studies and qualitative error analysis across arithmetic, QA, and commonsense reasoning benchmarks. Findings revealed structural limitations of inference-time reasoning in encoder-decoder architectures.
